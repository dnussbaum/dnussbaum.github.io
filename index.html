About Me

As a software developer, a digital strategist, and a Northwestern University student, I’m driven by a desire to make the world a better place. In my personal life, professional career, political advocacy, and nonprofit work, people count on me to solve challenging problems with creative solutions.

I’m deeply passionate about technology and advocacy. In high school, I interned for Brad Schneider’s 2012 campaign and acted as the digital coordinator for his 2014 re-election run. Additionally, I interned in advertising analytics at Organizing for Action, the policy advocacy offspring of the 2012 Obama campaign. Last summer, I built data management and analytics tools for Mothership Strategies, an email fundraising firm serving democratic campaigns and organizations.

At Northwestern, I’m the Technology and Analytics Co-Chair for our 30-Hour Dance Marathon, one of the largest student-run philanthropies in the nation. I’ve also served on the Executive Boards for Northwestern’s Investment Management Group and chapter of the Alpha Epsilon Pi fraternity.

I believe that innovative uses of technology can improve our world, not only combined with advocacy and philanthropy but also in our everyday lives and businesses. I want to be an agent for that change.
